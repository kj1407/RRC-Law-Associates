Strategic Quashing of Malicious and Frivolous FIRs

It is increasingly common for commercial competitors, estranged spouses, or rival family branches to file false criminal complaints (FIRs) against an NRI to extort financial settlements or force a return to India. Under Section 482 of the CrPC (and corresponding provisions of the unified criminal codes), the High Court holds inherent powers to strike down frivolous proceedings. Our criminal defense division specializes in preparing comprehensive quashing petitions before the Madras High Court, proving a lack of prima facie evidence and dismantling malicious prosecutions before they disrupt your life.

Securing Anticipatory Bail and Preventing Airport Arrests

The immediate risk an NRI faces upon learning of a domestic criminal charge is the threat of custodial arrest immediately upon landing at an Indian airport. To mitigate this vulnerability, our trial advocates initiate rapid applications for Anticipatory Bail before competent Sessions Courts and the High Court. We demonstrate the client's deep community ties, absolute willingness to cooperate with investigations via virtual video links, and highlight the malicious intent behind the complaint—granting you absolute immunity from arbitrary arrest.

Overcoming Lookout Circulars (LOC) and Passport Holds

When an NRI fails to respond to local police summons, investigative agencies routinely request the Ministry of Home Affairs to issue a Lookout Circular (LOC), triggering an automated red flag across all international airport immigration desks. If you are trapped abroad due to an active LOC or facing a passport impounding action under the Passports Act, immediate action is required. We file targeted Writ Petitions to quash arbitrary LOCs, negotiate conditional travel clearances, and resolve passport renewal holds smoothly.

A Lookout Circular (LOC) is an automated red-flag immigration restriction initiated by investigative agencies through the Ministry of Home Affairs when an individual fails to respond to official police summons. Under the Passports Act, regional passport authorities can place a hold or issue an impounding notice if a criminal matter is pending before a magistrate court in India. To resolve this, we file an explicit application before the trial court seeking directions for passport renewal.
